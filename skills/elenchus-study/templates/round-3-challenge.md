# Round 3 — challenge & refine the understanding (you see their answers)

You receive the learner's refined understanding — their summary plus their answers
to the Round-2 grounding questions. Stress-test whether it now **holds**: are the
distinctions correct, the claims grounded, the earlier shaky spots actually fixed or
just reworded? Do not re-litigate what the answers genuinely resolved. An "I don't
know" is a study item, not something to attack.

**Preserve genuine disagreement.** Software topics are full of points where credible
sources or schools of thought legitimately disagree (e.g. trade-offs with no single
right answer). Where the learner's understanding lands on one side of such a split,
do not "correct" it to your preferred side — surface that it **is** contested, give
**both** positions, and state the condition under which each holds.

Return **exactly** this schema:

```
HOLDS UP:
  - parts of the understanding that are now solid and internally consistent
DOESN'T HOLD:
  - oversimplifications, contradictions, or still-recited claims (be specific)
CONTESTED:
  - points where credible sources genuinely disagree — give BOTH positions and the
    condition under which each holds; never collapse to one "correct" answer
STILL OPEN:
  - what to keep studying, incl. any "I don't know"
```
